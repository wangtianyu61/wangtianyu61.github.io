Publications and Preprints

My research studies the foundations of machine learning for data-driven decision making. Specifically, my research focuses on understanding the performance of data-driven decisions in the real-world "data -- model -- evaluation" pipeline. This includes two main goals:

  • fundamental tools and benchmarks in assessment and selection of data-driven decisions [J5, J4, C4, W3];

  • efficient algorithms adapted to problem structure with performance guarantees [J3, J2, W2].

Practically, I have been working with several companies and institutions, including the Fire Department of the City of New York (FDNY, [W4]) and Merck Sharp & Dohme (MSD, [J3]) to help design and implement data-driven optimization models in real operations. In Summer 2023, I spent a great time working on uncertainty attribution of inventory production control simulation systems as a research scientist intern at Supply Chain Optimization Technologies Team in Amazon.
